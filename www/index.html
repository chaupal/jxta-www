JXTA Community Project

You can use Project JXTA technology to create peer-to-peer (P2P) applications based on Java technology. JXTA technology is a set of open protocols that enable any connected device on the network, ranging from cell phones and wireless PDAs to PCs and servers, to communicate and collaborate in a P2P manner. JXTA peers create a virtual network where any peer can interact with other peers and resources directly, even when some of the peers and resources are behind firewalls and network address translations (NATs) or on different network transports.

This project belongs to the JXTA Community, and supplements services and tools offered at our primary site jxta.org. Check out our many downloads, documentation sets, papers and, or course, projects available at jxta.org!

Project JXTA is fortunate to have a strong supportive community. Come join in the discussions and development efforts. There is such a wide range of activities to join in on that there is sure to be something of interest to you!


Join our JXTA discussions

There are general discussion lists where you can connect with other JXTA developers. These lists and our mailing list best practices are described at http://www.jxta.org/project/www/maillist.html. In addition, JXTA projects can have their own discussion lists, so check be sure to check those out as well.

If you are interested in contributing to our wiki page, please join in and share your ideas.
