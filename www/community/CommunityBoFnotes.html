·       Background on Project JXTA
Project JXTA was formed by Bill Joy and Mike Clary of Sun Microsystems.  The JXTA protocol was designed to provide a standard framework for interoperability among various peer-to-peer networks.  The JXTA shell application uses familiar UNIX commands.  JXTA defines a set of protocols for distributed environments.  The JXTA protocol was initially written in Java, but projects are under way to develop a C/C++ version.  Project JXTA is an open-source project (www.jxta.org), released on April 25, 2001, distributed under an Apache-like license.

·       Project JXTA Governance
The Board should contain three members:  one from Sun and two that are voted on by the community.  The Board should serve terms longer than six months. 
The Board should not have to get involved too often but should be exist to provide guidance and/or settle any disputes that may occur in the community. 
All community decisions should be made on the mailings lists and not at any particular community meeting.
