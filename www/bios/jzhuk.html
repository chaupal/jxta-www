Developer Spotlight


Jeff Zhuk has been a member of the JXTA Community since November 2003. You may know him as "jzhuk" on the JXTA Community discussion lists. He spends about 20% of his time working with JXTA Technology from his office in Denver, Colorado.

Working on the Knowledge Alliances project with advanced security (Patent Pending), he originally became interested in centralized (web-based) solutions, but knew that it would not scale for growing knowledge and networks. JXTA offered the way to distributed solutions. He also looked at Groove technology as another p2p solution. He chose JXTA Technologies because:

  • JXTA protocols have full potential for creating distributed self-healing networks
  • JXTA protocols include possibilities for multi-level privileges to access services and data
  • JXTA protocols and some JXTA implementations are open to the public

Jeff notes that JXTA can help us to move into a new world of distributed knowledge and services where new types of applications (and related jobs :-) will be available. He thinks that in many areas, like processing multiple forms of security data, or working in educational environment, where knowledge sharing and integration is extremely important, distributed knowledge networks is the best and probably the only real solution for growing complexity of information and related services. JXTA is a perfect fit for communications between students working on a team project, between students and instructors, between researchers that need to share knowledge and resources. He sees JXTA technologies as a potential mainstream solution for distributed network implementations.

He is also a member of OpenCyc.org, an ontology related project. OpenCyc is the open source version of Cyc technology, the world's largest and most complete general knowledge base and commonsense reasoning engine.

Jeff is the president of ITSchool, Inc. He is a software architect, educator, and developer with over twenty years of experience, and a speaker at Java One and Wireless One conferences. Jeff has numerous patents and publications, teaches at the University of Phoenix and at DeVry University, and conducts corporate consulting and training in XML, Java, WAP, Distributed Networks, and Knowledge Engineering. His recent book “Integration-Ready Architecture and Design” by Cambridge University Press bridges the gap for a new generation of software technologies and teaches a set of skills that are becoming extremely valuable today, and which will certainly be in high demand tomorrow. At the Java One International Conference Jeff will present a session on “Distributed life in JXTA knowledge networks".

A former mountain climber, he likes to ski and hike in the Rocky Mountains.
