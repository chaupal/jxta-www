Developer Spotlight


Xi Rao of Bloomington, Indiana is a first year PhD student in Computer Science at Indiana University. He has been a member of the JXTA community since May 2001 and dedicates 10-20 hours per week to Project JXTA. He is currently working on a research project directed by Geoffrey Fox and is responsible for integrating JXTA with a web brokering system written by Shrideep Pallickara.

Last year Xi Rao was interested in details pertaining to JXTA technology's Java-based reference implementation. He read a lot of the JXTA source code to familiarize himself with the strategies deployed in the implementation. This year he has been spending most of his time working on the integration of JXTA within the Grid Computing Laboratory at Indiana University's web brokering system. The area where Xi Rao finds himself spending most of the time during this integration process is the rendezvous layer. It will be the infrastructure for their collaborative distance education system and the Online Knowledge Center application. The integration of JXTA with their web brokering system will be an open source project once it passes the preliminary testing phase. Xi Rao has been involved in benchmarking the JXTA system as well as the integrated system.

Xi Rao views JXTA as a good infrastructure for connecting multitude of devices and building distributed applications. He believes that a powerful JXTA browser is needed for propagating JXTA technology even further.

In his spare time, Xi Rao enjoys reading source code pertaining to open source projects. He tries to achieve an understanding of the software architecture underlying the implementation. He also works with several friends to develop a framework for making server side programming easier. The goal of the framework under development is to handle database accesses and EJB programming and minimize JSP programming.

You can reach Xi Rao at xirao@cs.indiana.edu or on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list.
