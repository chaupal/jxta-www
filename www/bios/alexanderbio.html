Developer Spotlight


Alexander Carôt of Lübeck, Germany has been a member of the Project JXTA Community since October 2001. He is an international student of IT & Design at FH Lübeck, Germany / Danmarks-Designskole, (Copenhagen) and now resides in San Francisco, California.

Alexander is the Project Owner of the HALU project and a Contributer to the myJXTA and vop2p projects. Besides Alexander's technical studies, he's an active musican. His studies are called IT & Design to integrate the arts & IT. His main idea is to have a continental (and later an intercontinental) live music jam session with JXTA as the p2p core. Alexander is also interested in highly developed user-friendly GUIs for p2p systems of any kind.

Alexander is currently working on his internship at Spark Soundstudio in Emeryville, CA, where he is gaining valuable experience in audio-engineering, sounddesign, and videoediting, These things are combined with his own musical activities as a bass player in Jazz (upright bass), Rock, and Funkmusic (electric bass guitar).

All of these things go hand in hand with the SoundWIRE project at CCRMA (Stanford University) and the NMP project at the University of California, Berkeley, which both focus on having musicians play live "together" without being physically located in the same place. Alexander thinks that JXTA could be one of the leading p2p technologies because it offers all of the important features of future network architecture.

You can reach Alexander at Jazzalex@ccrma.stanford.edu or on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list.
