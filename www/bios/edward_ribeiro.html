Developer Spotlight



Edward Ribeiro is a second year MSc. student at University of Brasilia (Brazil), and also a software developer in a brazilian government institution. He has been interested in JXTA since its creation, but started to work on it in the beginning of 2005. In his research, he is building a distributed infrastructure to launch and control computational biology programs as BLAST, for example, among three universities. This infrastructure is based on JXTA because it is the most mature p2p model available and offer good flexibility. He has been spending at least 20 hours a week working on JXTA. This project has allowed him to get in touch with the JXTA community to report bugs and discuss the platform. His most recent contribution to the community was the updating of the wikipedia information about JXTA. In his free time, he likes to read, watch movies, and play with his four year old son, Bruno.

Read more about Edward in the java.net interview.
