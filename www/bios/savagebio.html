Developer Spotlight



David Savage has been a member of Project JXTA since April 2001. He's the project owner of the GO and JXRTL projects and active on the discuss@jxta.org mailing list. David is currently in the process of setting up a consultancy business to provide help and advice to organisations interested in using distributed computing to improve their operations.

David sees Project JXTA as a useful step towards standardizing the fragmented Peer-2-Peer market place. He says that the P2P architecture provides a scalable solution that will massively increase the computational power of services on the Internet, but which also can be used to offload work from the core network infrastructure, so allowing the network to grow again. Both innovation and standardization are key to this endeavor and Project JXTA provides just the right mix of both.

In the future he expects to see a greater use of distributed computation and content distribution in commercial applications. He says that JXTA will allow smaller less powerful devices to form dynamic groups with other potentially totally different devices to perform complex tasks. David also thinks that JXTA is well positioned to form a coherent solution to this problem due to its open-source nature and backing from major players in the computer industry, including Sun Microsystems and other companies mentioned in the companies spotlight.

David anticipates that a major challenge for Project JXTA will be in providing developers with applicable tools to allow them to build robust applications on top of the potentially chaotic P2P network topology.

Outside of the JXTA world, David has worked as a developer and architect for the telecoms, finance and consumer market sectors for four years. Prior to this he was a student at Cardiff University where he studied Physics and worked with his professor on his final year project to develop a ground-breaking method of studying quantum-scale electrical conductivity phenomena.

In his spare time, David enjoys reading, listening to a wide range of music, cooking (curries a speciality), socialising with friends, watching movies, and programming and playing games on computers.

You can reach David on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list or at dave@morganspenserconsulting.com.
