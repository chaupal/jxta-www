Developer Spotlight


Raymond Gao, also known as “raygao” within the JXTA Community is a JXTA community’s board member and the founding editor and publisher of P2P Journal. Active in the JXTA community, Ray is a project leader, a commentator, and an implementer.

Ray first learned about Project JXTA from Bill Joy’s speech at Sun’s STS symposium in Denver, February 2001. Shortly afterwards, he started to develop an in-depth interest in project JXTA after attending and speaking at JavaOne conference, June 2002 at San Francisco. During that conference, he visited project JXTA booth and spoke with several companies involved with P2P technology.

Ray is also the editor-in-chief for P2P Journal, a peer-reviewed technical journal dedicated to the peer-to-peer and parallel computing interests. P2P Journal is quite unique, because the journal cover pages always show unique pictures, either from Ray’s personal paintings or pictures taken during his journey to different parts of the world. It shows arts and sciences complimenting each other. For example, one issue’s cover (Sydney, Day & Night) is an oil-painting from his trip to Sydney, Australia. And, another issue shows the beautiful domes of the St. Basil Cathedral in Red Square Moscow.

He is the project owner of the http://commons.jxta.org/ project, which tries to make JXTA more practical and easier to learn for new developers. The project is a set of utility libraries, building on top of the existing JXTA core platform and following the tradition of Apache Commons project. Additionally, he had started several other projects, e.g. JXTA-GRID and Venezia-Gondola projects.

Right now, Ray is a program (chief) architect for Nokia. Previously, he was an e-commerce architect for Sun Microsystems, EDS, Raytheon, and Computer Associates, where he was responsible for architecting and producing blueprints for multi-million dollars projects. He has also spoken widely at technology conferences and published a number of papers. For example, in October 2004, he presented a paper at the Dagstuhl seminar in Germany.

Ray firmly believes that the JXTA community can grow by working with other open-source communities and providing construction blocks for enterprise-grade applications. The key to JXTA’s success may depend on how well our community works with existing technologies and other platforms.

He believes that the concept behind JXTA is sound and may provide a blueprint for the next generation Internet where virtual network overlays allow continuity with existing platforms and accommodate the exponential growth from new mobile devices connecting to Internet.

He has also many friends in the JXTA Community. That is an extra bonus!

Personal interests and hobbies

Ray Gao is a world traveler who likes to capture images of his experience in oil paintings. He also loves music and composition. He plays a beautiful green-colored electric violin. And, he also has a singing dog called Zooba.
