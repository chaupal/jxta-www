Developer Spotlight


Bruno Margerin of The Washington DC area works for Sciences Systems Applications, Inc. as a contractor for NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center. He is developing a prototype of Peer-to-Peer Satellite Data Distribution System. He thinks that it is a more functional, cost effective and efficient way of science data distribution that conventional centralized client-server systems.

The first sets of JXTA based tools are designed for Landsat 7 data sharing. These Landsat 7 scenes use a unique distribution license in which a fee is charged per ordered scene. Once delivered to you, the scene can be redistributed freely.

Bruno first started on the Gnutella protocol in his free time customizing a JAVA implementation of Gnutella called furi (from William W Wang) in Fall 2000; thus the name Satella (SATellite + GnuTELLA). He became a member of the jxta.org community in early May and developed a Prototype called "Satella Prototype Phase I" in September 2001. Phase I is a JXTA-based proof of concept and derivated from Instant P2P.

Phase II is under development and is a service based prototype providing the basic of semantic search of Landsat 7 data. Phase II will provide a Web based GUI, for administration, configuration and use. It has been designed to be turned into web services easily later on. Satella Phase II is expected to be distributed to the Landsat community for testing and evaluation on May 2002.

Bruno spends 80% of its time on the Satella project resulting in about 40% of its time on JTXA development. He has contributed to the Project JXTA: Java[tm] Programmers Guide by providing the Creating a Secure Peer Group section and contributes to the implementation of metadata support into the CMS project.

Further information on Bruno can be found on his page on the Project JXTA People project.
