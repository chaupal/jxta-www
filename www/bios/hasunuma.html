Developer Spotlight


Satoshi Hasunuma, also known as "hasunuma" on the JXTA mailing lists, is the Project Owner of jxta-monitor. He launched this project on 21 June 2004. He is also a Contributor to the platform project. He spends about 20% of his time working with JXTA technology. He is located in Nakacho.Musashinoshi, Tokyo, Japan.

When he first started developing P2P applications, he came right to JXTA technology. He was attracted because it allowed him to focus on developing his application since JXTA takes care of the P2P needs. Peer groups allowed him to easily create a virtual overlay network. Pipes allowed for simple communication between peers. He also feels that JXTA's language independence is an additional strength of the technology. Since JXTA technology is not completely dependent on a specific development language, developers have a choice of using Java or other languages. JXTA technology can be used on personal computers but embedded machines and various sensors can use JXTA advertisements to communicate. This scalability and platform diversity is another advantage of using JXTA technology.

The jxta-monitor tool was inspired by Yuuichi Sakuraba who is a coworker and also a developer at Yokogawa Electric Corporation. Sakuraba's support enabled Hasunuma to develop the tool and the project space. Hasunuma works at the Ubiquitous Field Computing Research Center at Yokogawa Electric Corporation.

When Hasunuma and Sakuraba were developing a P2P application, they realized how important it was to verify that the JXTA advertisement published correctly. They developed the JXTA-monitor tool to perform this check. They created this as a debugging tool for identifying and resolving advertisement problems. It visualizes the messages by capturing the various JXTA advertisements available on the network. JXTA-monitor allows a developer to watch changes to the network when new clients, such as a new embedded device, is added to the network. This enables rapid identification of issues that may arrive with addition of the client's design or configuration. Hasunuma and Sakuraba decided to offer the tool to JXTA community. They hope the tool will be widely adopted for debugging activities by Open Source developers.

When he is not developing software, he spends as much time as possible participating in outdoor sports including mountain climbing, fishing and watching stars etc.

Reported August 2004
