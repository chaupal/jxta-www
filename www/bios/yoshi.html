Developer Spotlight


Hirokazu Yoshinaga from Japan, is the Project Owner of JXCube. This project is a fully distributed collaborative application that supports group based communication, plug-ins tools or mediation service for synchronization, consistency, etc.

Hirokazu is known as "yoshinaga" on the JXTA discussion lists. He has been part of the JXTA Community since June 2001, working on JXTA Technology about 40% of his time. When he first started working with P2P technologies, he looked at Gnutella and Microsoft Windows XP P2P while first exploring Project JXTA. He found that Gnutella does not have good discovery scalability. Security at protocol level is an even bigger issue. On the other hand, Microsoft Windows XP P2P is IPv6 Internet Connection Firewall SDK, which not supported on most user environments. It also still contains many of the issues originally reported in IPv4. He found that JXTA, with its Overlay functions, offers more flexible functions than IPv6. After deliberating on the options, he ultimately selected JXTA Technology as his P2P strategy.

Hirokazu says JXTA technology fits in the current Internet environment that a lot of nodes exist in heterogeneous environment. That is, a variety of devices in different networks are able to communicate each other using JXTA overlay functions. He believes that this will lead to company-to-company or person-to-person interactions.

He differentiates JXTA technology from other P2P protocols not only because it is a client-based technology, but also because it is possible to integrate into server-based systems such as J2EE applications. Specifically, it will enable to extend the server systems easily due to the inherent scalability. And also, it will enable to construct fault tolerance systems simply by the flexibility in JXTA's discovery mechanisms. Hirokazu expects that P2P technology will become the new paradigm of networking. He also expects that JXTA Technologies will a focal point of this field.

He was previously an engineer in a software company. He is currently at the Graduate School of Information, Production, and Systems at Waseda University. He is also a writer for C Magazine, a Japanese journal for programmers. He has written a series of articles about P2P programing using JXTA. His primary areas of interest are P2P networking, Overlay networking, and Thinking networks.
