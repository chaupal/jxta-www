Developer Spotlight


James Todd, known as gonzo on the jxta.org aliases, is a Sun Microsystems employee based in Santa Clara, California. His role has predominately been to apply JXTA features with complimentary enterprise deployment capabilities like that of JNDI/LDAP, WebStart/JNLP, etc... He also considers himself the general rebel rouser. He is the owner of the myjxta2 and rosettaChat projects. He'd like to merge/coopt rosettaChat into myjxta2 as a service/feature.

James sees JXTA as the developer enabler to leverage resilient features, like those found in DNS and SendMail, directly within the application domain to:

  • uplift application features
  • provide pervasive deployments
  • increase ease of use.
Further he believes, JXTA is a principal means by which Internet/intranet based applications can escape the (limiting) four walls of the browser on devices large and small.

He has also helped roll out the initial release of Tomcat/Ant/WatchDog. James is a happy user of JDOM/Jaxen/Drools, Tomcat, Ant, and FreeTTS.

You can reach James at gonzo@jxta.org
