Developer Spotlight


Gilbert Maerina of Waimea, Hawaii has been a member of the Project JXTA community since March 2002. He is a committer on the Bench project and a contributor to Search, JXTA-RMI, and jxtacast. He dedicates 20 hour a week to Project JXTA, actively engaging in testing the scalability, robustness and performance of the technology.

Gilbert is an undergraduate Computer Science student with the University of Hawaii-Hilo and is expecting to graduate in the spring of 2004. He has interned at the Pacific Missile Range Facility of Barking Sands, Kauai, and was interning as a software developer for year with Digital Systems Resources-Hawaii Office until his change of status to junior software engineer in July of 2002.

Gilbert's vision is that JXTA will be great to create large ad-hoc communities. He also envisions using JXTA to create public/private distributed computing communities, JXTA SuperProcessing.

You can reach Gilbert at gmaerina@dsrnet.com or on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list.
