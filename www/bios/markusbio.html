Developer Spotlight


Markus Oliver Junginger started working with JXTA technology in early 2002 on behalf of his Master Thesis, in which he presents a Peer-to-Peer messaging system for scalable group communication. Although this system uses its own communication infrastructure, it incorporates JXTA features to look-up peers, and to find partitioned network segments. He is currently implementing further QoS parameters and a higher degree of intergration with JXTA.

For a German magazine on Java technology, he wrote a JXTA programming tutorial which is freely available online: article one and article two. .

In cooperation with Vivien Dollinger (www.yven.biz), he is developing a Peer-to-Peer collaboration tool, which will be released shortly. Vivien's web site provides further details and an early prototype. The availability will be announced at his web site www.junginger.biz that also offers information on other projects.

Markus Oliver recently finished his Computer Science MS studies (Best Student Award) and research assistantship at the University of Missouri - Kansas City. He is also a candidate for graduation (Diplom Informatiker, FH) at the University of Applied Sciences in Augsburg, Germany. In the second half of 2003, he will be working in industry.

You can reach Markus Oliver at markus@junginger.biz
