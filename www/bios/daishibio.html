Developer Spotlight


Daishi Kato is a researcher in the Computer Science department at Stanford University. He is also the Project Owner of Jnushare and GISP. He dedicates 10-20% of his time to Project JXTA. He's working on providing GISP in the JXTA service layer so that a JXTA application can easily achieve information (metadata) sharing in a peer-to-peer manner. GISP is based on distributed indexing which is potentially scalable. Daishi has also provided Jnushare, currently a file/message sharing application.

Daishi hopes that JXTA will become a common platform and be present in every computer. His vision is that there would be many peer-to-peer applications on JXTA connecting each other all over the Internet. Daishi says that one could find lots of information through JXTA network and hopes that in the future, with GISP, it would be possible to search for any information without search engines.

You can reach Daishi on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list or at daishi@axlight.com.
