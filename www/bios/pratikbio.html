Developer Spotlight


Pratik Dhebri of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania is a Master’s student at Carnegie Mellon University’s Information Networking Institute (INI). He has been a member of the Project JXTA community since January 2002 and dedicates 10-20 hours per week to JXTA. Pratik is a project owner of Angelo’s Peer Rendezvous; an enterprise based instant communicator which he developed at INI with the help of his peers using JXTA technology. He is also a contributor on the jxta.org P2P-email and security projects.

In addition to his work with JXTA technology, he is also the creator of Prasec (Pratik’s Security), a dynamic, totally distributed peer-to-peer based Public key Infrastructure. He hopes that other people will exploit the functionality of this platform and develop many applications using this architecture, avoiding the high costs associated with a central Certification Authority. Pratik is also active in the Bouncy Castle developer community and is helping the community by implementing Transport Layer Security in the Bouncy Castle Java Cryptographic Extension.

Pratik envisions peer-to-peer networking to be the next revolution in computer networks after the Internet. He thinks that an efficient, common platform like JXTA will facilitate rapid development of such applications and also help achieve interoperability between various peer-to-peer applications. He foresees a world where every device will be connected.

You can reach Pratik Dhebri at pratik@dhebri.com or on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list.
