Developer Spotlight


Robin Johnson is the owner of the JuxtaProse project and a committer on the CMS project. Approximately 60% of Robin's free time is dedicated to Project JXTA, mainly to JuxtaProse. It's a discussion application where all conversation and moderation is accomplished by sharing files using the content management system. While being a discussion application right now, it has the potential to become the P2P equivalent of the web browser by incorporating JXTA URLs into the messages and allowing pluggable URL handlers.

It seems obvious to Robin that a JXTA browser should be discussion-based because commenting on existing content and publishing new content is every bit as fundamental to the peer-to-peer paradigm as "consuming" content. Along with other projects, JuxtaProse requires advertisements with more metadata fields than are currently provided by CMS. This has led Robin to work towards improving the extensibility and interoperability of the metadata in CMS.

Robin says that after sadly watching the world wide web become increasingly subject to corporate and government control, JXTA is a very tempting fresh start. JXTA promises to be as versatile as the web, but decentralized enough to utilize the computing resources of the hundreds of millions of home computers connected to the Internet. Since the JXTA network would be powered largely by home users, they would have much more say in deciding the way the network is run, hopefully bringing back the notion of the Internet being a place where individual freedoms can thrive.

Robin recently graduated from a technical training program at the University of Minnesota and is currently looking for a UNIX system administration job. You can reach Robin on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list or at robin_j25@yahoo.com.
