Developer Spotlight



Bernard Traversat is currently the senior engineering manager for the JXTA and RFID engineering groups at Sun Microsystems. He is the Sun's representative to JXTA community board. He is the co-project owner of the JXTA-J2SE, the JXTA-C, and JXTA-J2ME project on jxta.org. He is spending most of his time lately on the JXTA-C and JXTA-J2ME implementations. Bernard co-authored the "JXTA in a Nutshell" book by O'Reilly Press. Previously he led Sun's effort in pervasive computing for small consumer devices, and was lead developer on the SunCluster product. Prior to that, he worked at the NASA Ames Research Center on distributed-memory operating systems for massively parallel supercomputers. He has numerous technical publications in distributed-memory operating systems and resource management systems. He received his Ph.D. degree from the Florida State University.

The simplicity of an asynchronous and secure decentralized peer-to-peer protocol to reconstruct the Internet is the brilliant idea of a visionary mind: Bill Joy. Bernard remembers clearly one afternoon. They were waiting for Bill to join the meeting from his Aspen's office for a video conference. They discussed different ideas for more than three hours. The same night at 2:00AM, Bill sent the group an email that defined the overall architecture of JXTA.

Since then, JXTA has been capturing the mind set of developers. Developers are using JXTA in many different ways to build applications that overcome today Internet limitations, and bring back the Internet to its origin: a highly-decentralized, scalable, and resilient network. Today government agencies, universities, research labs, and commercial companies are building all kinds of JXTA applications.

Bernard states "JXTA is also the dedicated work of a few engineers here at Sun and within the JXTA community that never doubted on what we were doing. I have learned to appreciate the values of working for an open-source project, and having people helped us implement the JXTA vision.

This has been pure joy for me to work on JXTA ;-)"

You can reach Bernard at tra@jxta.org
