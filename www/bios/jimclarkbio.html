Developer Spotlight


Jim Clark is the owner of the JxtaView Discovery Demo project and a committer on the shell, myJXTA, and RossettaChat projects. He dedicates approximately 30% of his time to Project JXTA.

According to Jim, the JxtaView Demo project is mostly his "fault". It demonstrates the JXTA discovery process by allowing the user to manually launch discovery, and then showing the results in a graphical tree display of groups and peers. The JxtaView Demo download also includes a demo of RosettaChat running within myJXTA. It's available from the JxtaView homepage: http://jxtaview.jxta.org

Jim is currently working on a picture sharing demo. PicShare is a JXTA application that broadcasts pictures to fellow peergroup members. Each peer displays a picture frame. When you drop an image file into the frame, it is sent to all the other peers in the group that are running PicShare, and will appear in their picture frames. More information and screen shots from a UI prototype are available at http://nightware.home.mindspring.com/picshare/picshare.html

Jim would like to see JXTA become the industry standard development platform for peer-to-peer applications. He says that the ultimate goal would be for JXTA to become so simple, stable, and easy to use that it becomes an almost taken-for-granted part of the development process, the way a good user interface library is now.

Jim is an independent contract programmer, of the Jack-of-All-Trades variety. He's available for contract work on JXTA or other projects. You can reach him on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list or at nightware@mindspring.com
