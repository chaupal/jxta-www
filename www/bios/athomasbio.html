Developer Spotlight


Athomas Goldberg of New York is the President and CTO of Improv Technologies, Inc. and Chief Architect of Improv's Cirquet Platform, a component-based framework for developing, deploying and managing large-scale distributed applications based on JXTA. He has been a member of the Project JXTA community since April 2001 and dedicates 45-55% of his time to Project JXTA.

Athomas is the Project Owner for many projects on jxta.org: jaas-membership, jnlp-loader, lrmp-endpoint, and validation-agent. In addition, he's a committer on the platform, soap and gameplatform modules. His principal contributions to Project JXTA have been in trying to provide enterprise-level services on top of the core platform, as well as trying to ensure that the platform itself meets the needs of enterprise scale applications. In addition, Athomas is active in the Jini developer community as well as various groups devoted to intelligent agent development.

Athomas sees the P2P network embodied by Project JXTA to be the next real evolutionary step for the Internet and the Web. He says that the client-server model that dominates the Internet can never match the flexibility and scalability an architecture like JXTA's provides. As JXTA matures he believes that we will begin to see the distributed applications deployed on a global-scale, connecting everything from the largest super-computers to the smallest hand-held devices and appliances.

Athomas doesn't think the challenge for JXTA is finding the killer-app, because he thinks the world is full of distributed applications inefficiently deployed on out-dated centralized architectures. When you compare the cost and effort of deploying and maintaining applications in a traditional manner versus a peer-to-peer approach like JXTA, the benefits are obvious. He says that the real challenge for JXTA will be in making these applications easily accessible to the average user in the same way that J2EE made client-server applications easily accessible to anyone with a computer, an internet connection and a web browser.

You can reach Athomas on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list or at atgoldberg@improv-tech.com
