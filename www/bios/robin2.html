Developer Spotlight


Robin Johnson is known as rmjohnson within the JXTA Community. He lives in Durango, Colorado and has been working with JXTA technologies since June 2001. He is involved in several different JXTA Community Projects. He is a Committer on Allhands project, a Committer on CMS project, and the Owner of JuxtaProse project.

He currently dedicates spends about 20% of his work time working with JXTA technology. He is currently working for Syndicom, Inc. which is developing a case tracking system for spine surgeons called SpineConnect. They had started the Allhands project with the goal of creating a generalized case-tracking platform which could be customized to build more specialized applications, like SpineConnect. Prior to becoming involved with Syndicom, he had started the JuxtaProse project, which is the discussion application on which Allhands is based.

When Robin was first designing the system that would become project JuxtaProse, he considered using Gnutella technology. Once he discovered JXTA technology, he was totally hooked. He felt that JXTA appears to be the most (if not the only) open extensible platform for general peer-to-peer networking. He found the Gnutella protocol is far less versatile when it comes to offering additional services beyond filesharing and simple searching. He hopes to see JXTA become a rich set of building blocks for quickly creating a custom peer-to-peer system as easily as one would create a centralized system.

Since the HTML interface of Allhands is built using Jakarta Tapestry, Robin has been involved in the Tapestry community in addition to his activities within the JXTA Community. When he is away from his computer, he enjoys mountain biking, trail running, hiking, skiing and snowboarding.
