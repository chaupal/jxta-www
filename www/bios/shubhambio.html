Developer Spotlight


Shubham Tyagi of Buffalo, NY has been a member of the JXTA Community since September 2001. He is an Undergraduate Student in Computer Engineering at SUNY Buffalo. In the Fall of 2002 he will be a Graduate Student in Computer Science at the University of Southern California, Los Angeles. Shubham's areas of interest is Distributed Systems.

Shubham is the project owner of jxAuction and dedicates approximately 30-50 hours a week to Project JXTA. Over the last few months he has been working on a commercial auction application that uses the JXTA backbone for all communication. His goal is to setup a free and reliable peer-to-peer auction network free from moderators.

Shubham thinks that JXTA is going to play a major role in the development of future Distributed Systems and Peer to Peer applications. The fact that it uses XML for communication and is compatible with multiple protocols like tcp, http, etc. gives developers a great convenience. Once all the stability and security issues have been resolved, Shubham sees the internet almost free of servers and dependent on peer-to-peer applications that allow users to share resources and do infinite other things.

You can reach Shubham at tyagi@buffalo.edu or on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list.
