Developer Spotlight

 

Elizabeth (Betsy) A. Nichols or "ean", by her jxta.org username, has been a member of the JXTA Community since December 2003. She is CTO of Sequation, Inc. a company pioneering security metrics management. She is located in Vienna, Virginia. She currently spends approximately 10% of her time working with JXTA technology. She has been participating in the development of JXTA technology by sharing the findings of Sequation developers with the JXTA community, while developing a complex distributed security management application.

When Sequation were first designing the SOS application and researching appropriate technologies to use, they considered using Globus. They decided to use JXTA technology because of the simplicity of design and based their design on the Java-based implementation. Betsy cites benefits to her development team as:

    1. We do not have to implement the functionality ourselves
    2. We are part of a larger community that is both testing and deploying in large environments as well as improving the technology
    3. We expect the protocols will become a broadly accepted standard
    4. JXTA can support peers written in languages other than Java

Betsy has a personal vision for how peer-to-peer and grid technologies can be used to support the functionality required for IT security and risk management. Specifically, she believes that any measurement and control of risk must result from the fusion of information across multiple, heterogeneous sources. These sources must know about assets, their configuration, their current operational state, their vulnerabilities, countermeasures or defenses, and finally threats -- potential or future. For this reason, risk management is a process that requires collaboration and cooperation of multiple "specialists" -- software based and human, embodied as services in a service oriented architecture. These services must operate as peers to derive a risk state and then take appropriate action to protect and/or defend. The best architecture for this is a peer-to-peer grid of cooperating services -- in stark contrast with the manager-agent hierarchies that were embraced by earlier 20th Century systems management technologies.

She continues that the peer-to-peer architecture is not enough. There is an additional requirement for distributed service workflow orchestration and an associated application development environment to facilitate creation of security and risk management solutions on an enterprise wide scale. This is the value that Sequation has layered on top of JXTA. SOS Release 2.4 is currently shipping.

Betsy is a founding member of the Java Management Extension (JMX) expert group within the JCP. Sequation is a strong believer in opensource philosophy. They develop using opensource software from a number of different organizations.
