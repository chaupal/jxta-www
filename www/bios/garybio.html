Developer Spotlight


Gary Orser of Bozeman, Montana has been a member of the JXTA Community since September 2001. He is a Senior Systems and Software Engineer for the Center for Computational Biology in the Cell Biology and Neuroscience Department at Montana State University. He is currently a contributor to the platform and jxta-wire projects, and spends 10-20 hours per week working on the JXTA project. He has extensive experience using many open source projects at the University since 1996, coupled with extensive unix experience since 1987. The JXTA project is his first experience contributing to an open source project.

Gary is currently working on a distributed peer-to-peer application to allow research scientists to collaborate with each other, using a self mediated database to describe, query and share extensive meta-data about experimental data and the experimental setup of their scientific research. This project, when it reaches alpha status, will be an open source project. It is leveraging several open source projects, including both JXTA and Xindice.

Gary believes that peer-to-peer applications will be, in time, the driving force to enable scientists to more effectively collaborate, drive adoption of standards, and allow scientists to "do better Science".

You can reach Gary at orser@cns.montana.edu or on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list.
