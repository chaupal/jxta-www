Developer Spotlight


Christine Karman is the Founder of Tryllian in Amsterdam, Holland. Tryllian, which has been a member of the Project JXTA community since May 2001, provides distributed agent products, services and solutions. Christine is an astrophysicist by training, is a longtime software engineer who has been working in artificial intelligence since 1987 and mobile-agent technology since 1993. A pioneer in artificial intelligence, she left her position at the Dutch software company, Origin, and founded Tryllian in March 1998. She recently relinquished her CEO title in spring 2001, however she remains actively involved with the company, focusing on strategic business development and public relations.

Christine thinks that the world has been thinking in terms of centralized systems for decades, then everyone turned to client-server, which provided as little flexibility as centralized systems. She says that what we need is flexibility and adaptivity. Users don't want to know about architectures. Users need data, information, knowledge, and communications wherever they are. Christine sees JXTA as a step towards extremely flexible and adaptive architectures.

Christine says that JXTA enables Tryllian to use auto-discovery, pass firewalls, and solve their NAT problem. They have been using JXTA since the summer of 2001, and now are actively using the security features. JXTA allowed them to solve infrastructural problems their very dynamic environment poses, enabling Tryllian to focus on the core features of their platform.

You can reach Christine at christine@tryllian.com or on the discuss mailing list.
