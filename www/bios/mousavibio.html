Developer Spotlight


S. M. Mousavi Jahan Abadi is a software architect at Digital Dream, Inc. in Tokushima, Japan and has been a member of the Project JXTA community since May 2002. He received a Ph.D. in Systems Engineering, focused on distributed systems and multi-agent models for supply-chain management. Currently he's working on Ifreestyle, a personal information manager and team-collaboration software product based on JXTA technology. Mousavi dedicates approximately 10-20 hours per week to Project JXTA as a contributor to the Platform and Bench projects.

In his work on JXTA and Ifreestyle, Mousavi mainly concentrates on mechanisms for real-time event notification, online and offline database synchronization, distributed search, and detection of member presence. He has been working on the Xindce native XML database too. He believes that P2P networks are necessary complementary technology for server-client models, and could change the future of our communication and collaboration over distributed systems in the real-world. Mousavi sees JXTA as an outstanding and well-designed implementation for distributed systems, providing various services for different needs. He believes that future JXTA projects should provide modular and tiny solutions for different needs of P2P networks, which can be easily plugged into any application or device.

You can reach Mousavi at mousavi@digitaldream.co.jp or on the Project JXTA discuss mailing list.
